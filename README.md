## Project Overview
This project develops a publicly accessible interactive analytics dashboard to explore long-term Canadian labour market trends in employment, wages, and sustainability. The dashboard evaluates Canada's labour market outcomes from 1976 to 2026 and situates them within the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth) framework.

## Motivation
Canada's labour market has undergone major structural change, from deindustrialization in the 1980s to the COVID-19 shock and the ongoing green economic transition. Existing tools do not combine long-term Canadian labour data, wage trends, SDG 8 benchmarking, and green economy analysis in one unified system.

## Objectives
- Analyze long-term employment trends across industries and provinces
- Examine wage quality and regional wage differences
- Benchmark Canada against peer countries using OECD and UN SDG data
- Explore how green economy transition relates to labour market outcomes
- Deliver a reproducible data pipeline and dashboard for analysis and presentation

## Data Sources
- **Statistics Canada - Labour Force Survey (LFS)**: employment by industry and province
- **Statistics Canada - SEPH**: average weekly earnings by province and industry
- **OECD Annual Wages Dataset**: annual wages for Canada and peer countries
- **UN SDG Goal 8 Indicators Dataset**: SDG 8.5.1, 8.5.2, and 8.6.1 indicators
